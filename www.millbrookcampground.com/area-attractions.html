Hiking Trails

 

The Long Trail

 

Carved in Vermont by the Green Mountain Club between 1910 and 1930, the Long Trail, that extends along eastern United States, is the oldest long-distance hiking trail in the United States. The Long Trail travails along the main ridge of the Green Mountains from the Massachusetts-Vermont line to the Canadian border as it crosses Vermont's highest peaks. It inspired the Appalachian Trail, which coincides with it for one hundred miles in the southern third of the state. Long Trail Guide

 

The Long Trail passes through Montgomery, Vermont, before bringing hikers to traverse Jay Peak. From there, the Long Trail extends into North Troy, where its sojourn as a United States hiking trail ends.

The Jay Peak Resort

 

​

 

Visit the Jay Peak Resort in Jay, VT, a year round resort, that caters to many visitors during the summer months for fun and adventure.

 

The Pump House Water Park, a one of a kind water park, is an indoor adventure. Enjoy surfing, water tubbing and much more.

 

Hike to the top during the spring, summer and fall or ride The Tram that will take you on a mountainous journey.

 

Their PGA Championship Golf Course is a one of a kind, with beautiful views, challenging terrain and located in a beautiful setting.
